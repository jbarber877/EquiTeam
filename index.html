Clean and Affordable Energy

The UN goal of affordable and clean energy is part of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), specifically Goal 7: Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all.

  • Next

Energy Access for All

Universal access to modern energy services by 2030. Currently, many people, especially in developing regions, still rely on traditional biomass (e.g., wood, charcoal) for cooking and heating, which can be inefficient and harmful to health. The goal is to ensure that everyone has access to clean and modern forms of energy, like electricity and clean cooking technologies.

Increase Renewable Energy Share

Significantly increase the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix. Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power are crucial to reducing greenhouse gas emissions, improving energy security, and creating sustainable energy systems.

Energy Efficiency

Double the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency by 2030. Energy efficiency improvements reduce energy waste, lower costs, and reduce environmental impacts. Efficient appliances, buildings, and industrial processes are examples of areas where significant gains can be made.

Sustainable Infrastructure

Expand infrastructure and upgrade technology for supplying modern and sustainable energy services in developing countries. Context: Developing nations often face inadequate infrastructure that prevents them from expanding access to reliable and affordable energy. Investment in infrastructure and technology upgrades can help bridge this gap.
